Where Homebase Falls Short

The gaps that matter most for field-based and multi-location businesses.

📡

GPS that doesn't actually verify location

Homebase records where an employee was when they clocked in but doesn't enforce geofencing. Employees can clock in from a parking lot, a coffee shop, or home — and the system won't stop them. WorkTrack's geofencing blocks clock-ins outside the designated zone.

📵

Offline mode that doesn't work

Homebase advertises offline functionality but user reviews consistently report error messages when there's no internet connection. For construction crews, landscapers, and anyone working in dead zones, this is a dealbreaker. WorkTrack handles offline clock-ins and syncs when connectivity returns.

📍

Per-location pricing punishes multi-site businesses

Homebase charges per location, not per company. If you run 3 locations, you pay 3x. For a business expanding from 1 to 3 locations, your Homebase bill triples overnight. WorkTrack's flat rate covers your whole operation — no matter how many sites you add.

🔨

No job costing or task tracking

Homebase is built for retail and restaurant shift scheduling. It doesn't support assigning time to specific jobs or projects — a must-have for contractors, cleaners, landscapers, and service businesses that bill by job. WorkTrack includes job costing at every plan level.

When Homebase Is the Right Choice

We'll be straight with you.

Homebase is genuinely good at shift scheduling for fixed-location businesses — restaurants, retail shops, salons. If you have one location, predictable shifts, and no need for GPS verification or job costing, Homebase's free tier may be enough.

WorkTrack is the better fit if you have field employees, multiple locations, job-based billing, or you're tired of paying monthly fees that grow with your headcount. The flat-rate model means the price you pay today is the price you pay in year three.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions when comparing Homebase and WorkTrack.

Is Homebase really free?

Homebase has a free tier for one location with basic clock-in/out. But it lacks GPS enforcement, offline mode, job costing, and overtime alerts. Most businesses that need those features end up on a paid plan at $20–$80/month. WorkTrack starts at $497 one-time with all core features included.

Does Homebase have GPS time tracking?

Homebase records location at clock-in but does not enforce geofencing — employees can clock in from anywhere. WorkTrack includes full GPS geofencing that blocks clock-ins outside a defined job site radius, preventing buddy punching and off-site punches.

Does Homebase work offline?

Not reliably. Multiple user reviews report that Homebase's offline mode fails and serves error messages when there's no internet connection. WorkTrack is built to handle offline clock-ins and syncs automatically when the device reconnects — critical for field workers.

How does WorkTrack pricing compare to Homebase for multiple locations?

Homebase charges per location. Two locations = 2x the monthly fee. Three locations = 3x. WorkTrack charges a flat one-time rate that covers your entire operation regardless of how many locations you have.

What are the main reasons businesses switch from Homebase to WorkTrack?

The most common reasons: (1) GPS that doesn't actually enforce geofencing, (2) offline mode that fails in the field, (3) no job costing or task scheduling, (4) per-location pricing that multiplies as you grow, and (5) wanting a flat rate with no ongoing monthly fees.
