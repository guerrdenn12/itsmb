Why People Leave QuickBooks Time

Pulled from real user reviews on G2 and Capterra.

💸

Pricing that punishes growth

Every time you hire, your bill goes up. At $10/user/month, adding 5 employees adds $600/year — forever. Most SMBs realize this is backward when they're trying to scale.

🔗

Hidden dependency on QuickBooks Online

TSheets was sold as standalone but works best (and is often sold) with QuickBooks Online — another $30–$200/month subscription on top of the per-user fee. Many users don't realize this until after signup.

📱

Mobile app quality dropped after Intuit acquisition

Since Intuit acquired TSheets and rebranded it, user reviews consistently flag freezing, crashes, and GPS tracking errors on the mobile app. Field-first businesses are hit hardest.

📞

Support that feels like a big company

Multiple Capterra and G2 reviews describe spending hours on hold or waiting days for responses. When payroll depends on your time tracking, that's not acceptable for a small business.

Frequently Asked Questions

The questions we get most from businesses comparing options.

Is WorkTrack cheaper than QuickBooks Time (TSheets)?

Yes — significantly. WorkTrack uses flat-rate pricing starting at $497 one-time. QuickBooks Time charges $20/month base plus $10/user/month. For a 20-person team that's $440/month or $5,280/year. Over 3 years, WorkTrack saves over $15,000 on that same team size.

Do I need a QuickBooks subscription to use QuickBooks Time?

QuickBooks Time technically works standalone, but it's built to sync with QuickBooks Online — which costs $30–$200/month extra. WorkTrack has no external subscription dependency.

Does WorkTrack have GPS time tracking like TSheets?

Yes. WorkTrack includes GPS clock-in/out, geofencing, and real-time location for field employees — all in the flat-rate price. No per-user upsell for GPS features.

Can WorkTrack handle job costing?

Yes. WorkTrack lets you assign time entries to specific jobs or projects so you can see labor costs per client or job site — useful for contractors, landscapers, and service businesses that bill by project.

What if I'm already using QuickBooks for payroll?

WorkTrack exports payroll data in CSV format compatible with QuickBooks and other payroll tools. You can keep using QuickBooks for accounting — you just don't need QuickBooks Time (or its monthly fee) for time tracking.

What are the main reasons businesses switch from TSheets to WorkTrack?

The top reasons: (1) per-user pricing that grows every time you hire, (2) needing a QuickBooks Online subscription, (3) mobile app instability after the Intuit acquisition, and (4) slow support response times when payroll depends on accuracy.

The Verdict

If you're running a service business with hourly employees and you want GPS tracking, job costing, and overtime alerts — without paying per user every single month — WorkTrack is the smarter choice for most SMBs.
